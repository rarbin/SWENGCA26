\subsection*{Definition}
\addcontentsline{toc}{subsection}{Definition}
A user persona imagines a specific type of user for a given platform. e.g. SETUP, a student services app, may consider students, lecturers, and/or guests. Product expectations may vary depending on user background, education and skillset.\\ 

By conceptualising the types of users this product seeks to attract; personas assess what features are best to proritise. It also suggests what features may require further development, or scrapping entirely, in order for the product to be suitable for its demographics.

\subsection*{2. Connor Byrne, a Department of Science lecturer}
\addcontentsline{toc}{subsection}{Persona 2}
Byrne is a 42 year old lecturer in the Department of Science at SETU. He specifically lecturers in classes relating to Biology or Pharmaceuticals. With over 10 years of experience in teaching in this field, he delivers modules to undergraduates, supervises final year research projects, and contributes to the research conducted by the university. \\

Byrne manages large amounts of students across different groups each semester, often trying to balance lecturing, grading, meetings, research deadlines, and to stay in touch with students who may have questions. Byrne finds students contacting him across a plethera of platforms, most being Slack, Moodle, Outlook, Teams, etc. He finds that switching between these apps to keep track of messages is time-consuming and inefficient. \\

Byrne would benefit from an app like SETUP, which allows for all these platforms to be confined to a central hub. This would also help him manage schedueling meetings with students and staff, grading, exam timetables, as these can be tasks that are done automatically. Reducing the amount of repetitive tasks and simplifying it down to a reporting process would significantly improve his workflow. \\

Seemless integration and local navigation of all the socail platforms that the university uses will allow him to stay up-to-date and in frequent contact with his research group, fellow staff, and all the students that attempt to reach out to him. \\

\subsection*{4. Jane, an accounting student with accessibility concerns}
\addcontentsline{toc}{subsection}{Persona 4}

Jane is a 20 year old first year undergraduate, pursuing a BA (Hons) in Accounting at SETU. She has been receiving good grades and is an active member of the SETU snowboarding society. She commutes two hours by bus from Wexford for her 9am-5pm weekly classes. \\

She has experienced difficulty in navigating the various grants and accomodation forms, and would appreciate an app that makes booking appointments with services such as MABS, counselling or the disability office more straightforward. \\


ADHD can cause Jane to become disorganised with her appointments, because of this she keeps a physical journal. Unfortunately, she does not always remember to keep this journal updated, so having an automated digital calendar  for academic and social events  would be useful. \\

Jane also uses a low vision setting on her phone for app navigation, she's noticed a lack of support in many online services for this feature. SETUP should consider the accessibility needs of users like Jane when developing upon its services.\\
