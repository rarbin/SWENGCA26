\subsection*{Product Vision}
\addcontentsline{toc}{subsection}{Product Vision}
As students of SETU, we aim to develop a dedicated, accessible student services product, that unifies the apps and utilities common to daily student life. The ideal SETU Personalised (SETUP) application integrates both existing and new educational and social features alike, giving staff \& students more control over their educational experience, in real time, in one place. \\

To gain a better understanding of what we want SETUP to be, we investigated three existing products; Maynooth University App, xTiles \& Studo. While each differs in scope and functionality, all serve as a reference point for different features SETUP implements. \\

\subsection*{A2. xTiles}
\addcontentsline{toc}{subsection}{A2. xTiles}

\textbf{1. FOR}
Students and creative professionals, especially those seeking accessibility features. \\

\textbf{2. WHO}
aim to visualise their workflow with a variety of templates and widgets. \\

\textbf{3. XTILES} is a \textbf{workspace and visual productivity platform} that is aimed towards daily planning, personal tasks, note-taking and project management.\cite{xtiles}  \\

\textbf{4. THAT}
Features drag-and-drop like moving texts, links and images that make it easy to rearrange
your workspace. It is user-friendly and suitable for creating schedules and weekly study schedules. As a target demographic, students are provided special offers on paid plans.

\textbf{5. UNLIKE} 
document-esque formatted alternatives, e.g. Microsoft Office 365, xTiles offers an at-a-glance overview of its users tasks, schedules and notes. First time users may find the UI of accessibility features provided by xTiles to be less intimidating for this reason. The lack of download or specific device requirements also make acess of the platform convenient, provided a user has reliable internet access.\\

\textbf{6. XTILES} design approach succeeds not on any one feature alone, but rather by providing its own visual spin on the popular features found across existing similar platforms;\\
\begin{itemize}
    \vspace{-0.5\baselineskip}
\item \textbf{Flexible} visual workspace where you can customize to your own needs.\\ (Enables the user to add texts, images, links, etc.)
\item \textbf{Real-time} multi-user support for collaboration.\\ (Teams can work on a board together.)
\item \textbf{Utilities} for making plans, brainstorms and organising.
\item \textbf{Cross-platform}, accessible through multiple devices.
\item \textbf{Integration} with other applications e.g. Google Calendar.\\ (Allows events and deadlines to be synced across project.)
\item  \textbf{Templates} provided, giving users the option of customisation without the expectation\\ of starting from scratch.
\item Visually appealing and \textbf{intuitive UI} improves upon \textbf{accessibility} and engagement \\for ADHD, low-vision, etc.
\end{itemize}

When taking inspiration for the SETUP app, it's worth considering the reported limitations\cite{xtrev} of the platform:
\begin{itemize}
\item  Lacking in \textbf{offline support}, making it unreliable for those without consistent internet.
\item The large variety in tools can be offputting to new users without \textbf{clear instructions}. 
\item \textbf{Limited free plan} often pushes users into paid alternatives to access what some may expect to be basic features.
\end{itemize}

It's difficult for any project to predict how any given feature will be received or what challenges it may bring to development. For this reason, being receptive to user feedback at all stages of development can be hugely beneficial in the long-term. \\

\textbf{5. UNLIKE} separated, smaller scope student services, which develop their platforms independently, e.g. Moodle/Outlook. Studo is not in competition with so much as it serves to integrate existing developed services into a convenient front-end interface for its user. \\

\textbf{6. STUDO} distinguishes itself by focusing development on the following features:\\
\begin{itemize}
    \vspace{-0.5\baselineskip}
  \item \textbf{All-in-one} interface for daily university/on-campus services \\(email, calendar, grades, timetables)
  \item \textbf{Real-Time} polls and push alerts
  \item \textbf{Easy integration} for existing records and student information
  \item \textbf{Accessible} course and grade overview. \\ (highlights dates for assignments and uploads)
  \item \textbf{Synced} cross-platform from multiple devices
  \item University news feeds, vouchers, lunch menus \\
\end{itemize}

However, when considering its design for a SETUP application, note it's often criticised for: 
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Limited} or \textbf{redundant} features, many of which are heavily admin restricted.
  \item \textbf{Lack of customisation} options for students
  \item Chat feature between students is heavily moderated and \textbf{overly censorious}. 
  \item 2FA and login features are unreliable, reported difficulty in students connecting accounts among other \textbf{integration issues}.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Initial Features}
\addcontentsline{toc}{subsection}{Features}


Taking inspiration from the product vision cases above, we can use our analysis to form the foundation for the SETUP App's feature categories. \\

Many listed features exist in some form within the SETU website's student hub, meaning the infrastructure for supporting timetables, lab results, etc. will not have to be majorly altered to support the app.\\

\textbf{Academic}\\
Services related to a student's specific course.
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textit{Timetables}\\
         Timetable provided based on the details of a logged in user, eliminating the need to manually input details by default as seen on the existing timetable webpage.
  \item \textit{Exam/Lab Results}\\
         Using moodle integration, congregate a students relevant lab results in the one place. Provide useful stats such as overall average, overall percent for a given lab, etc.
  \item \textit{Academic Calendar}\\
        Relevant university events, allow editing for individual students to add their own information similar to a study planner.
   \item \textit{To-Do/Notes Tool}\\
         Utility for quick notes, tracking of various deadlines set by Moodle, templates for study schedules.
  \item \textit{Examinations}\\
        Upcoming student examinations, room details, etc.
  \item \textit{Moodle Integration}\\
        In a similar fashion to Studo, integrate Moodle as a tab within the app directly. 
  \item \textit{Email Integration}\\
        Allow users to connect several email accounts, e.g. outlook, gmail, to the one inbox.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Student Life} \\
Services related to a student's social life, not necessary course specific.
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textit{Clubs \& Societies}\\
         Information on various clubs and societies, alongside options to pay membership.
  \item \textit{News \& Events}\\
         University updates, events, news. Opt-in follow to be notified for specific niches e.g. Computer Science Department events. 
  \item \textit{Transport Schedules}\\
        Bus/train commute info, could be integrated with services such as TFI.
  \item \textit{Library}\\
        Options to reserve/renew loaned books, organise study pods, check opening hours, etc.
  \item \textit{Campus Map}\\
        Integrated Google Map of the SETU campus, options to add custom markers, notes, etc. to buildings.
  \end{itemize}
\textbf{Support Services} \\
University support services/utilities, grants \& fee management. 
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textit{Grants \& Fees}\\
        Grant application info, student fee details, may be ideal to include information on external grants such as SUSI. 
  \item \textit{Disability Support}\\
        Contact info and associated systems for the disability service.
 \item \textit{Counselling Services}\\
        Booking and associated systems for the counselling service.
  \item \textit{Computer Services}\\
        Booking and associated systems for the computer service.
   \item \textit{Eduroam \& Printing Utilities}\\
        Wifi and printer setup.
  \end{itemize}

\textbf{User Experience}\\
Broad considerations when implementing the above services.
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textit{Intuitive Design}\\
        Menus and layout should be concise and visually clear. Integrated first or third-party features should not be unnecessarily bloated or redundant.
  \item \textit{Cross-Platform Sync}\\
        Sync a user across devices, (desktop, phone, etc.) and the SETU website when internet is available.
  \item \textit{Offline Access}\\
        Allow users to save info such as timetables, lab results, etc. for use offline.
  \item \textit{Feedback \& Reports}\\
        Users should be able to report bugs, criticism or suggestions on improving the app.
  \item \textit{Accessibility Tools}\\
       Settings for low-vision, colour-blind, screen-readers, etc. 
  \item \textit{Free and Efficient}\\
       Assuming development is supported by SETU, advertisements or paid premium memberships should not be expected. Exceptions for society memberships, student tuition fees.
  \item \textit{Real-time Notifications}\\
       Users may opt-in to various push notifications, updates, etc.
   \item \textit{GDPR compliant; data secure}\\
       Use of 2FA, privacy options, secure cloud storage handled by services like Cloudflare, Google Drive. Payment services also trusted to a third party such as stripe/google pay.
  \end{itemize}
